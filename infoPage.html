Reduce. Reuse. Recycle.

Learn how reducing, reusing, and recycling can help you,

your community, and the environment by saving money, energy,

and natural resources.


Reusing refers to using an item again, whether for its original purpose or a new one.

Reducing waste means finding ways to use less materials and energy in order to minimize waste and consume fewer natural resources.

Recycling is the process of collecting and processing materials that would otherwise be thrown away as trash and turning them into new products.

Your Right To Know

  • How to reduce?

    1. Repair, not discard
    2. Cancelling unnecessary mail
    3. Not using disposable plates or utensils at home
    4. Avoid to buy plastic water bottles or plastic bags
    5. Don't overbuy perishables at the grocery store (meal planning can help with avoiding wasted food)

  • How to reuse?

    1. Using a washable rag to clean your house instead of paper towels
    2. Donating old clothes or household items
    3. Buying second-hand
    4. Using your old bedding for your pet’s bed
    5. Use reusable shopping bags or totes instead of plastic
    6. Repair broken appliances, furniture and toys
    7. Buy a reusable water bottle instead of buying bottled water

  • How to recycle?

    The main barrier to recycling is understanding the process. That means knowing where and what to recycle. Each city has their own regulations and processes for recycling. Make sure you do your research.

  • Where to recycle?

    There are a number of places where you can easily recycle, specially arranged places in several points in each city.

  • What to recycle?

    There are a few materials that cannot be recycled like garbage, food waste, windows and mirrors, ceramics, food-tainted items, etc. The most common recyclable materials are:
    1. Paper
    2. Plastic
    3. Glass
    4. Metals
    5. Batteries
    6. Electronics

  • Tips for recycling:

    1. Make sure it’s clean! One single dirty product can contaminate an entire bale. It can ultimately cause recyclable items to get redirected into a landfill
    2. Flatten cardboard. This will save space in your bin and make room for more recycling
    3. Set up designated recycling containers at home, work, and school
    4. Look for products made out of recycled materials when you shop
    5. Before you recycle, think about reducing your waste and re-using items in a new way

  • Facts about recycling:

    1. 10 plastic bottles can save enough energy to power a laptop for more than 25 hours
    2. Recycling 1 ton of office paper can save the energy equivalent of consuming 322 gallons of gasoline
    3. Recycling one ton of aluminum cans conserves more than 152 million BTUs, which is the equivalent of 1,024 gallons of gas or 21 barrels of oil
    4. Recycling one glass bottle saves enough energy to light a 100-watt lightbulb for four hours
    5. One ton of recycled cardboard saves 46 gallons of oil

The 3 'R's

The principle of reducing waste, reusing and recycling resources

and products is often called the "3Rs."

All of these help to cut down the amount of waste we throw away.

They conserve natural resources, landfill space and energy. Plus, the three R's

save land and money that communities must use to dispose of waste in landfills.
